### 1. Executive Summary

This document outlines the product requirements for the **Wholesale Business Dashboard**, a modern, responsive web application designed for wholesale company owners. The primary goal is to provide a single, at-a-glance view of the business's operational health. By consolidating key metrics for sales, orders, inventory, and customers into an intuitive, scannable interface, the dashboard will empower owners to make faster, more informed decisions without navigating complex spreadsheets or legacy systems. This initial version (MVP) will focus on a read-only experience, establishing a strong foundation for future analytical and transactional capabilities.

### 2. Problem Statement

Wholesale business owners are often overwhelmed by the sheer volume of operational data scattered across different systems (e.g., accounting software, warehouse management systems, order sheets). They lack a simple, centralized way to get a quick pulse on their business. Answering fundamental questions like "How are we performing this month?", "Which orders need my attention?", or "Are we about to run out of a key product?" requires manually compiling reports, which is time-consuming, inefficient, and prone to error. This delay in accessing critical information can lead to missed opportunities, poor inventory management, and a reactive, rather than proactive, approach to running the business.

**User Pain Points:**
*   **Data Overload:** Information is fragmented and difficult to synthesize.
*   **Time Consuming:** Manually pulling reports takes valuable time away from strategic activities.
*   **Lack of Immediacy:** By the time data is compiled, it may already be outdated.
*   **High Cognitive Load:** Complex interfaces make it hard to spot trends and anomalies quickly.

### 3. User Personas & Stories

#### 3.1. Primary Persona

*   **Name:** Sarah Chen
*   **Role:** Owner of "Artisan Goods Wholesale"
*   **Description:** Sarah is a hands-on owner of a mid-sized wholesale business. She is tech-savvy but time-poor. She spends her day managing supplier relationships, overseeing a small team, and ensuring customer satisfaction. She typically checks her business health first thing in the morning on her laptop and periodically throughout the day on her tablet or phone.
*   **Goal:** "I need to know the state of my business in under 60 seconds so I can identify what needs my immediate attention today."

#### 3.2. User Stories

**Authentication:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to log in securely with my email and password **so that** my business data remains private and protected.
*   **As Sarah,** I want the system to keep me logged in **so that** I don't have to re-enter my credentials every time I open the dashboard.

**Dashboard Overview:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see all my key business metrics on a single screen **so that** I can get a complete operational overview without clicking around.
*   **As Sarah,** I want the dashboard to load quickly and display correctly on my desktop, tablet, and phone **so that** I can stay informed whether I'm in the office or on the go.

**Sales Performance:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see my total revenue and profit for the last 30 days **so that** I can quickly gauge my financial performance.
*   **As Sarah,** I want to view a chart of my daily sales over the past month **so that** I can identify trends and spikes in activity.
*   **As Sarah,** I want to know my Average Order Value (AOV) **so that** I can understand if my customers are increasing their purchase sizes.

**Order Activity:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see a list of the 5 most recent orders **so that** I am aware of the latest sales activity.
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see a summary of orders by status (e.g., Pending, Shipped, Delivered) **so that** I can quickly identify bottlenecks in my fulfillment process.

**Inventory Status:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see a list of my top 5 lowest-stock items **so that** I can prioritize reordering and avoid stockouts.
*   **As Sarah,** I want to know my top 5 best-selling products by revenue **so that** I understand what is driving my business.

**Customer Insights:**
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see a list of my top 5 customers by total spend **so that** I can recognize and nurture my most valuable relationships.
*   **As Sarah,** I want to see a breakdown of new vs. returning customers this month **so that** I can assess my customer acquisition and retention efforts.

### 4. Functional Requirements

#### 4.1. User Authentication
*   **FR1.1:** The application must provide a login page with fields for email and password.
*   **FR1.2:** Authentication will be handled via a credential provider (e.g., NextAuth.js).
*   **FR1.3:** The dashboard page and its associated API routes must be protected, requiring a valid user session to access.
*   **FR1.4:** A "Log Out" button must be present to allow the user to terminate their session.

#### 4.2. Dashboard Layout
*   **FR2.1:** The main dashboard will be a single page.
*   **FR2.2:** The layout will use a responsive grid system to organize widgets.
*   **FR2.3:** On desktop, widgets will be arranged in a multi-column layout. On mobile, widgets will stack vertically in a single column.
*   **FR2.4:** A clear, consistent header will display the application name/logo and the "Log Out" button.

#### 4.3. Dashboard Widgets
*All widgets must display a loading state while data is being fetched and an error state if the data fails to load.*

*   **FR3.1: Sales Performance Widget**
    *   **KPI Cards:**
        *   Total Revenue (Last 30 days)
        *   Total Profit (Last 30 days)
        *   Average Order Value (AOV) (Last 30 days)
    *   **Visualization:**
        *   A line chart displaying daily revenue for the last 30 days.
        *   Hovering over a point on the chart will display the date and revenue for that day.

*   **FR3.2: Order Activity Widget**
    *   **Summary:**
        *   Display counts for orders with `Pending`, `Shipped`, and `Delivered` statuses.
    *   **Table: "Recent Orders"**
        *   Display the 5 most recent orders.
        *   Columns: Order ID, Customer Name, Order Date, Total Amount, Status.
        *   The status should be a color-coded tag/pill for quick scanning (e.g., Yellow for Pending, Blue for Shipped, Green for Delivered).

*   **FR3.3: Inventory Status Widget**
    *   **List: "Low Stock Items"**
        *   Display the top 5 products with the lowest stock quantity.
        *   For each item, show: Product Name, SKU, and Quantity Remaining.
    *   **List: "Top Selling Products"**
        *   Display the top 5 products by revenue generated in the last 30 days.
        *   For each item, show: Product Name and Total Revenue.

*   **FR3.4: Customer Insights Widget**
    *   **List: "Top Customers"**
        *   Display the top 5 customers by total spend in the last 90 days.
        *   For each customer, show: Customer Name and Total Spend.
    *   **Visualization:**
        *   A simple donut chart or KPI cards showing the count of New vs. Returning customers in the last 30 days.

### 5. Non-Functional Requirements

*   **NFR1: Performance**
    *   The dashboard page must achieve a First Contentful Paint (FCP) of under 2 seconds.
    *   The total page load time (on a standard connection) must be under 3 seconds.
    *   The application must achieve a Google Lighthouse performance score of 85 or higher.
*   **NFR2: Usability & Accessibility**
    *   The UI must be modern, clean, and uncluttered.
    *   All interactive elements must have clear hover and focus states.
    *   The application must follow WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines for color contrast and keyboard navigation.
*   **NFR3: Responsiveness**
    *   The application must be fully functional and visually polished on the following viewport widths:
        *   Mobile: 375px
        *   Tablet: 768px
        *   Desktop: 1280px+
*   **NFR4: Security**
    *   User passwords must be hashed and salted before being stored in the database.
    *   All data transmission between the client and server must be over HTTPS.
*   **NFR5: Reliability**
    *   API endpoints should have a 99.9% uptime.
    *   The frontend should gracefully handle API errors and display user-friendly messages.
